title: "How to handle Trial Reels"
description: "Learn how to handle Instagram trial reels in Sheer."
---

Trial reels let you handle Instagram Reels that are experimental so they don't affect your performance metrics. Trial reels are excluded from analytics, keeping your numbers clean and representative.

## Why handle trial reels?

Sometimes you want to test a new format, hook, or topic without it skewing your analytics. Any reel
handled as a trial is excluded from your performance data and shown with a Trial Reel badge in the UI.

## How to mark a reel as a trial reel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Performance">
    Navigate to your **Creator Profile**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a reel">
    Click on the reel you want to mark as a trial.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit the reel">
    In the detail modal, click **Edit**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark as trial">
    Check the **Trial reel** checkbox at the bottom of the form and click **Save**.

    <Frame caption="The Trial reel checkbox is found at the bottom of the reel edit modal, just above the Save button.">
      <img src="/images/trial-reel-checkbox.png" alt="Reel edit modal showing the Trial reel checkbox" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

The reel will now display a **Trial Reel** badge and be excluded from your analytics.

## How to view trial reels

By default, trial reels are hidden from the performance view. To see them:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Performance">
    Navigate to the **Performance** tab on your Creator Profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open filters">
    Open the filter/custom view popover.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle trial reels">
    Turn on the **Trial reels** switch to include them in the view.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Good to know

- Trial reels only apply to **Reels**. Not posts, stories, or other media types.
- Reels must be **manually tagged** as trials. Currently they are not detected automatically.
- Tagging is **reversible**. Incheck the checkbox to restore the reel to normal status.
- Only the creator (or a talent manager) can tag their own reels.
